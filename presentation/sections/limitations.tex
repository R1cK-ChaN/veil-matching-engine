\section{Limitations and Future Work}

\begin{frame}{Limitations \& Next Steps}

\begin{columns}[T]
\column{0.49\textwidth}
\begin{block}{L1 --- Full-fill only}
Equal-quantity matching avoids reactivating remainders. Real books split orders.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{L2 --- No cancellation}
Orders only leave by being matched. Cancel needs its own transition and frame conditions.
\end{block}

\column{0.49\textwidth}
\begin{block}{L3 --- Frame, not global}
Unrelated traders unaffected, but $\sum \texttt{cash}$ and $\sum \texttt{asset}$ conservation not yet discharged.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{L4 --- Relational, not refined}
\texttt{isBestBid}, \texttt{isBestAsk} are predicates, not data structures.
\end{block}
\end{columns}

\vspace{2mm}

\begin{block}{The most consequential next step}
\textbf{Refinement.} Show that a sorted-book implementation $I$ \emph{refines} the abstract spec $A$ --- closing the gap between ``the rule is right'' and ``the engine implements the rule.''
\end{block}

\end{frame}
