\section{Background: Price-Time Priority}

\begin{frame}{The Single Legal Matching Rule}

A continuous limit order book is governed by \alert{price-time priority}.

\begin{block}{Best Bid / Best Ask}
$b$ is \emph{best bid} iff no active bid has higher $px$, or equal $px$ with earlier $ts$.\\
$a$ is \emph{best ask} iff no active ask has lower $px$, or equal $px$ with earlier $ts$.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{Legal Match}
\[
\begin{aligned}
  & \texttt{isBestBid}(b)\ \wedge\ \texttt{isBestAsk}(a)\ \wedge \\
  & \texttt{bidPx}(b) \geq \texttt{askPx}(a)\ \wedge\ \texttt{buyer}(b) \neq \texttt{seller}(a).
\end{aligned}
\]
\end{block}

\textbf{Encoding.} \texttt{isBestBid}, \texttt{isBestAsk} are \emph{relational predicates} --- abstract, refinable.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Worked Example: A Small Order Book}

Bids prefer higher prices; asks prefer lower; equal prices break by earlier $ts$. \alert{Best} order on each side is highlighted.

\begin{columns}[T]

\begin{block}{Cross \& execution price}

\end{block}
