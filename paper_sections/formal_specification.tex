\section{Formal Specification}

\subsection{Book Ordering}

The buy side is ordered by descending price, with earlier timestamps breaking
ties. The sell side is ordered by ascending price, again with earlier
timestamps breaking ties. These orderings induce the local notion of
price-time priority used throughout the proof.

\subsection{Execution Rule}

The matching step considers the best live bid and best live ask. If the two
orders are compatible, the system produces a trade record, updates account
balances, and removes or decreases the matched orders according to the chosen
fill model. In the minimal version, full fills only are acceptable if they
lead to a significantly cleaner proof argument.

\subsection{Trade Log and Account State}

The trade log is part of the formal state rather than an external trace. This
makes trade provenance properties easier to state and prove: each new trade
entry should be justifiable from the immediately preceding live-book state.
Account balances are also explicit in the state so that conservation can be
stated as an invariant over reachable states.
