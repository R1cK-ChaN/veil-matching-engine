\section{Safety Properties}

The main safety properties to prove are:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item \emph{stable book}: after matching completes, the remaining book is not
crossed;
\item \emph{price-time priority}: matching consumes the best available orders,
and within a price level it respects FIFO order;
\item \emph{conservation}: total cash and total asset quantity across all
accounts are preserved;
\item \emph{trade provenance}: every trade log entry can be traced back to two
orders that were live immediately before that step; and
\item \emph{quantity consistency}: no execution uses more quantity than the two
matched orders actually hold.
\end{itemize}

These properties are intentionally safety-only. The paper does not attempt to
prove liveness, throughput, or fairness beyond queue discipline inside the
matching rule.
