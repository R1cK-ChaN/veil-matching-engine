\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}

The paper intentionally abstracts away many features of real exchanges,
including multiple assets, market orders, protocol-level sequencing, complex
order types, and operational resilience mechanisms. This is a conscious scope
decision rather than an omission by accident. The aim is to obtain a clean,
non-trivial verified core, not to replicate every operational detail of a
production trading venue.

Another limitation is that some stronger claims may only be checked on bounded
instances if they are too expensive to prove deductively in the available time.
If that happens, the fallback is to prove a weaker but still meaningful local
property such as: each \texttt{match} step picks the current best bid and ask.
