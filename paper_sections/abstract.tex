This paper studies a simplified electronic limit-order matching engine and
formalises it as a transition system in Veil. The model focuses on a
single-asset order book with limit orders, explicit book ordering, trade-log
updates, and per-account cash and asset holdings. The goal is to prove core
safety properties expected of a price-time-priority exchange, including
queue-discipline correctness, conservation of cash and assets, and trade
provenance. The paper also outlines a bounded verification strategy based on
small-instance model checking, Veil invariant checking, and auxiliary Lean
lemmas for sortedness and arithmetic.
