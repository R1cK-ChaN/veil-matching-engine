This paper formalises a simplified electronic limit-order matching engine as a
transition system in Veil. The model covers a single-asset order book with
limit orders, trade-log updates, and per-account cash and asset holdings.
Matching is specified relationally through first-order best-bid and best-ask
predicates rather than by verifying a concrete book-processing algorithm.
Seven safety properties are proved, covering best-order selection, trade-field
provenance, no self-dealing, and a frame-style balance conservation property
for uninvolved traders. All eighty-four deductive invariant checks pass, and a
bounded model check over a small finite instance finds no violations.
