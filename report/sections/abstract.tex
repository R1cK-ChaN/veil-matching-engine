This paper studies a simplified electronic limit-order matching engine and
formalises it as a transition system in Veil. The model focuses on a
single-asset order book with limit orders, explicit order submission
parameters, trade-log updates, and per-account cash and asset holdings. The
verified artifact is an abstract legal-match transition system rather than a
proved concrete matching algorithm over a data structure. The goal is to prove
core safety properties for that abstract model, including best-order
selection, trade-field provenance, and balance-related frame properties. The
paper also reports a small bounded model-checking smoke test and the
declarative invariant checks used to validate the model.
