\section{Verification Results}

\subsection{Purpose}

The experiments in this project are verification experiments rather than
systems-performance experiments. Their role is to validate the specification,
surface counterexamples early, and document the scope of the checks used
during development.

\subsection{Deductive Invariant Checking}

The main result comes from Veil's deductive invariant checking. The current
model declares seven safety properties and thirteen supporting invariants.
Together with the generated \texttt{doesNotThrow} obligation, this gives
twenty-one checks per verification block. Veil checks initialisation and each
of the three actions (\texttt{doMatch}, \texttt{submit\_sell}, and
\texttt{submit\_buy}), producing eighty-four preservation checks in total. All
eighty-four pass.

\subsection{Bounded Model Checking}

A bounded model check serves as a smoke test over a small finite instance.
The bound fixes two traders (\texttt{Fin~2}) and three order identifiers
(\texttt{Fin~3}), with constant initial balances for both cash and assets.
Veil searches this finite state space for a violation of the declared safety
and invariant set and finds none. The explored-state count reported by Veil is
one, confirming that the finite domain is very small; the check is useful for
catching specification typos but does not substitute for the deductive proof.

\subsection{Interpretation}

The deductive checks establish that the declared safety and invariant set is
preserved by initialisation and all three actions in the unbounded model. The
bounded model check complements this as a sanity check but its scope is too
narrow to support claims about larger bounded price and quantity domains. Taken
together, the two results validate the specification at the level of the
abstract relational model presented in this paper.
