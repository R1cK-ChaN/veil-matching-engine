\section{Safety Properties}

The main safety properties to prove are:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item \emph{best-order selection}: every enabled match step uses an order
identified as the current best bid and the current best ask;
\item \emph{trade-field provenance}: recorded trade price and quantity are tied
to the matched ask and bid;
\item \emph{no self-dealing}: the matched buyer and seller are distinct;
\item \emph{frame-style balance preservation}: traders uninvolved in any trade
retain their initial balances; and
\item \emph{quantity consistency}: no execution uses more quantity than the two
matched orders actually hold.
\end{itemize}

These properties are intentionally safety-only. The current model does not yet
prove a stable-book theorem after repeated matching, nor a global
sum-conservation theorem over all traders, and it does not attempt liveness,
throughput, or fairness beyond the best-order selection built into the match
relation.
