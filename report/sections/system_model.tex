\section{System Model}

\subsection{Scope}

The model covers a single asset and limit orders only. Each order carries an
identifier, account, side, limit price, quantity, and timestamp. Only
submission and matching transitions are modelled; cancellation and partial
fills are excluded because they substantially increase the amount of
residual-state reasoning required without changing the core safety argument.

\subsection{State}

The engine state consists of a buy-side order book, a sell-side order book, a
trade log, per-account cash and asset holdings, and auxiliary bookkeeping for
timestamps and order identity. For proof convenience the current
implementation uses a relational encoding rather than sorted sequences: the
state records order membership and fields directly, and ghost predicates
define what it means for an order to be the current best bid or best ask.

\subsection{Transitions}

The transition system has three actions. \texttt{submit\_buy} and
\texttt{submit\_sell} add a new limit order to the appropriate side of the
book, assigning its price, quantity, and a monotonically increasing timestamp.
Both actions share a common freshness precondition: the order identifier must
not already appear in either book or in any existing trade record.
\texttt{doMatch} executes one legal trade between the current best bid and
best ask when their prices cross and their quantities are equal. It removes
the matched orders, updates the relevant account balances, and appends a trade
record.
