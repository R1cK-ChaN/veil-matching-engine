\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}

The paper intentionally abstracts away many features of real exchanges,
including multiple assets, market orders, protocol-level sequencing, complex
order types, and operational resilience mechanisms. This is a conscious scope
decision rather than an omission by accident. The aim is to obtain a clean,
non-trivial verified core, not to replicate every operational detail of a
production trading venue.

The current artifact is an abstract legal-match transition system rather than a
verified executable matching algorithm over an explicit data structure. In
particular, matching correctness is expressed through best-order predicates in
the transition preconditions, not by verifying a concrete book-processing
procedure.

Another important limitation is that the current accounting statement is weaker
than full global conservation: the proved invariant is a frame-style property
for traders uninvolved in all trades, not a sum-preservation theorem over all
accounts. Likewise, the bounded model-checking result is a very small smoke
test over finite traders and order IDs, not a broad exploration over bounded
price and quantity domains.
