\section{Limitations and Future Work}

The model intentionally abstracts away many features of real exchanges:
multiple assets, market orders, cancellation, partial fills, protocol-level
sequencing, and operational resilience. The aim is a clean verified core rather
than a replica of a production trading venue.

Because the specification uses a relational encoding, matching correctness is
expressed through best-order predicates in the transition preconditions rather
than by verifying a concrete book-processing procedure. Extending the work to
a verified executable matching loop---for instance via Velvet---would
strengthen the result by connecting the abstract specification to a runnable
algorithm.

The accounting result is weaker than full global conservation. The proved
invariant is a frame-style property for traders uninvolved in all recorded
trades, not a sum-preservation theorem over all accounts. Expressing the
latter requires quantifying over a summation that falls outside the
first-order fragment supported by Veil's SMT backend.

Finally, the bounded model check covers only two traders and three order
identifiers. A broader sweep over bounded price and quantity domains would
improve confidence in the specification but was not pursued in this iteration.
